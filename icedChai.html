Iced Chai

The Starbucks Iced Chai is a classic drink. It's adapted for the holidays as well, for example the Iced Pumpkin Chai debuts in fall, followed by the Iced Gingerbread Oatmilk Chai. It's popularity is earned, with its delicious flavor and caffeinated boost.

Here, you can learn a simple dupe that will taste just as amazing as Starbucks at a fraction of the cost. A Chai latte typically is made from equal parts chai concentrate, and a milk of your choice.

Ingredients

  • Your favorite chai tea bags - 4 single serving bags
  • Brown Sugar - 1/4 cup
  • Boiling water - 2 cups
  • Your favorite milk

Optional

  • Cinnamon
  • All spice

Instructions

  1. First, let your tea bags steep in the boiling water over the stovetop, for about five minutes. Don't oversteep, as the tea could become bitter.
  2. Next, put combine the brown sugar and stir untill it's dissolved. Allow to simmer for three minutes.
  3. Cool and store your concentrate in a secure container.
  4. Add the concentrate to equal parts of your favorite milk, and store extra in the fridge.
  5. Finally, just add ice and any desired spices to finish off the perfect Iced Chai
